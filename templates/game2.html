Genre: Psychological Horror

Released: 2001

Developer: Team Silent

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

My personal Review:

Silent Hill 2 is one of those stories that feels almost too perfect for the genre it lives in. The way it builds suspense isn’t cheap or loud — it’s emotional, slow, and painfully human, pulling you deeper into James’ guilt long before you even understand what he’s running from. The representation of grief, denial, and self‑punishment is honestly unmatched, and every monster feels like a reflection of something James refuses to face. What makes it even more brilliant is how the game lets you decide what kind of person James becomes through its different endings — whether he accepts the truth, collapses under it, or tries to rewrite it entirely. Each ending feels like a different version of the same tragedy, and that’s what makes the story hit so hard. Silent Hill 2 isn’t just a horror game; it’s a psychological mirror, and it leaves you thinking long after the credits roll.
